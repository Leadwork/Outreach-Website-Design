Every cold email campaign lives or dies by data quality. You can write the sharpest copy in the world, but if 30% of your sends bounce, you've already poisoned the domain before the prospect saw a subject line.

After running outbound for clients across SaaS, agencies, and managed services, I've stress-tested every major email finder on the market. Below is the honest list — what actually works, what's overpriced, and where each tool fits in a real stack.

This isn't a feature dump. It's a buying guide written for people who have to hit pipeline numbers next quarter.

## How to evaluate an email finder

Before naming names, here's what actually matters when you're picking a vendor:

- **Verified accuracy** — measured against a real bounce test, not the vendor's own marketing number.
- **Catch-all handling** — does it flag risky catch-all domains or just hand you a green checkmark and walk away?
- **Credit economics** — cost per *verified* email, not cost per "found."
- **Enrichment depth** — title, seniority, department, LinkedIn URL, tech stack.
- **API + native integrations** — Clay, Smartlead, Instantly, HubSpot, Salesforce.

> Rule of thumb: if a vendor won't let you run a sample test of 500 contacts before signing, walk away.

## 1. Apollo.io

Apollo is the default starting point for most teams, and for good reason. Its database sits at around **275M contacts** with strong filters for technographics, headcount changes, and intent.

- **Accuracy:** ~88–92% on verified emails for US/UK markets.
- **Pricing:** Starts $49/user/mo; serious users land on the $99 tier.
- **Best for:** Mid-market and SMB prospecting where you want filters + sequencer in one place.

Weakness: catch-all flagging is mediocre. Always pipe Apollo exports through a second verifier.

## 2. Hunter.io

Hunter is the cleanest single-purpose finder on the market. It does one thing — find and verify emails from a domain or name — and does it well.

- **Accuracy:** ~90–94% on confirmed-deliverable results.
- **Pricing:** $34–$104/mo depending on credits.
- **Best for:** Founders, small teams, and anyone running domain-based prospecting from a list of target companies.

Bonus: the Chrome extension is the fastest way to grab a single email mid-research.

## 3. Snov.io

Snov.io punches above its price tier. It includes finder, verifier, drip campaigns, and a CRM in one platform.

- **Accuracy:** ~85–88%.
- **Pricing:** Free tier with 50 credits; paid plans from $39/mo.
- **Best for:** Solo founders or lean agencies who want one tool to cover finding + sending.

Catch: deliverability of Snov's built-in sender lags behind Smartlead or Instantly. Use it for finding, then route sends elsewhere.

## 4. RocketReach

RocketReach has the deepest **direct-dial phone coverage** of the tools on this list, which makes it the go-to when you're running multichannel outbound (email + cold calls).

- **Accuracy:** ~80–85% on emails; ~70% on mobile numbers.
- **Pricing:** $80–$300/mo per seat.
- **Best for:** SDR teams that combine cold email with phone touches into enterprise accounts.

## 5. FindThatLead

FindThatLead is the budget pick for high-volume prospecting. Lower accuracy than Hunter, but credits are cheap.

- **Accuracy:** ~78–82%.
- **Pricing:** From $49/mo for 5,000 credits.
- **Best for:** Volume plays where you're already verifying with a second tool (NeverBounce, ZeroBounce) downstream.

## 6. Skrapp

Skrapp is built around the LinkedIn extension. Drop into Sales Navigator, build a list, and Skrapp pulls emails as you scroll.

- **Accuracy:** ~82–86%.
- **Pricing:** $49–$199/mo.
- **Best for:** Reps who live in LinkedIn Sales Nav and want a finder that matches the workflow.

## 7. GetProspect

GetProspect is the underdog. The UI is dated, but the LinkedIn extension and the bulk finder are reliable.

- **Accuracy:** ~84–88%.
- **Pricing:** Free tier, paid from $49/mo.
- **Best for:** Bulk LinkedIn list pulls when you don't want to pay Apollo prices.

## 8. Lusha

Lusha's reputation comes from **direct mobile numbers**, especially for European and US accounts. Emails are solid too.

- **Accuracy:** ~88–92% on emails; strong on mobiles.
- **Pricing:** $39–$69/user/mo; enterprise on request.
- **Best for:** Founder-led sales and account-based plays where you need the personal mobile, not the switchboard.

## 9. ContactOut

ContactOut shines at finding **personal emails** (gmail, hotmail, etc.), which matters when you're targeting executives who screen their work inbox or who recently changed jobs.

- **Accuracy:** ~85% on personal; ~90% on work emails.
- **Pricing:** $39–$199/mo.
- **Best for:** Recruiters, founder outreach to C-level, and anyone working accounts where the work email gets filtered into oblivion.

## 10. Voila Norbert

Voila Norbert is old-school and reliable. It's not flashy, but it consistently verifies and the pay-as-you-go pricing is friendly for occasional users.

- **Accuracy:** ~87–90%.
- **Pricing:** From $49/mo, or pay-as-you-go at $0.015/credit.
- **Best for:** Agencies running one-off list builds for clients, or anyone who hates monthly commitments.

## The stack I actually recommend

If you asked me to set this up for a client tomorrow, here's the stack:

1. **Apollo** for filtering, segmentation, and initial pulls.
2. **Hunter** for high-priority accounts where accuracy matters more than speed.
3. **Lusha or ContactOut** layered in for the top 200 accounts (mobiles + personal emails).
4. **MillionVerifier or NeverBounce** as a final verification pass on every list — no exceptions.

**Never trust a single vendor's verification.** Always re-verify externally before sending. A 2% bounce rate can melt a brand-new sending domain in a week.

## What to do this week

If you're rebuilding your data stack:

- Pull a sample of 500 contacts from your current provider.
- Run them through a second verifier and measure the real bounce rate.
- If it's above 3%, your provider is selling you stale data — switch.
- Set up a monthly hygiene audit so you catch decay before it costs you a domain.
