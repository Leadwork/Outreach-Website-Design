This guide is different in two ways. First, it's honest — we include ourselves (Pro Lead Maker) but we are upfront about exactly where each competitor beats us. We compiled this from public pricing, Clutch and G2 reviews, and our own knowledge of the space as operators. Let's get into it.

## How to choose a cold email agency (read this first)

Before the list, the five things that actually matter:

1. **Channel fit** — Do you need email only, or email + LinkedIn + calling? Single-channel agencies are cheaper; omnichannel costs more.
2. **Deal size math** — Outbound works when your average contract value is roughly $5K+ annually. Below that, cost-per-meeting can outrun the deal.
3. **Pricing model** — Flat retainer (predictable), pay-per-meeting (performance), or hybrid. Watch for hidden setup fees and tool costs on top of the retainer.
4. **Who runs your account** — A big agency may assign a junior manager juggling 15 clients. A boutique may give you a senior operator. Ask directly.
5. **Honesty about outcomes** — Be skeptical of guaranteed meeting numbers. No agency fully controls meeting volume; the honest ones guarantee the *system*, not an arbitrary number.

## The 10 best cold email agencies in 2026

### 1. Belkins — best overall for funded companies

The most-reviewed cold email agency on the market (4.9/5 on Clutch, 230+ reviews) and the safe enterprise pick. Founded in 2017, US-headquartered with a globally distributed team running email, LinkedIn, and calling as coordinated campaigns.

- **Strengths**: refined, productised methodology; serious deliverability infrastructure; fast onboarding; clear reporting.
- **Watch-outs**: premium pricing ($4,500–$8,000/mo); at their scale you may get a more templated experience than a boutique.
- **Pick them if**: you're funded, want a proven brand, and budget isn't the constraint.

### 2. SalesHive — best for US-based personalization at scale

A scalable US-based operation known for transparency and its eMod personalization engine, which pulls public data to write hyper-personalized emails at volume. Reports 115,000+ meetings booked across 250+ clients.

- **Strengths**: US-based talent; strong personalization tech; infrastructure that holds at volume.
- **Watch-outs**: email + calling focus, lighter on LinkedIn; mid-to-high pricing ($4,000–$7,000/mo).
- **Pick them if**: you want US-based SDRs and personalization at scale.

### 3. CIENCE — best for data-driven omnichannel

CIENCE differentiates on data and runs true omnichannel — email, phone, LinkedIn, chat, and ads — with managed, co-managed, and staffing models.

- **Strengths**: deep data operation; flexible engagement models; omnichannel breadth.
- **Watch-outs**: $5,000 one-time GTM setup ($2,500 for startups) plus ongoing fees; the breadth can be overkill if you only need email.
- **Pick them if**: you want a data-led, multi-channel program and can invest in setup.

### 4. Martal Group — best for technical B2B

Active since 2009, Martal serves B2B tech companies where the buyer is an engineer, CTO, or technical evaluator, with SDRs trained on technical categories.

- **Strengths**: deep-tech vertical expertise; multi-channel; long track record.
- **Watch-outs**: requires a 3-month pilot before month-to-month; $3,000–$8,000/mo.
- **Pick them if**: you sell a technical product to technical buyers.

### 5. Cleverly — best for LinkedIn-first outreach

The LinkedIn specialist, with 10,000+ clients served and email as a supplement. Entry pricing starts around $397/month, making it accessible for smaller teams.

- **Strengths**: deepest LinkedIn track record; self-serve dashboard; low entry price.
- **Watch-outs**: LinkedIn-first means email is secondary; templating across a huge client base.
- **Pick them if**: LinkedIn is the majority of your strategy. (We compare in depth in our [Pro Lead Maker vs Cleverly guide](/blog/pro-lead-maker-vs-cleverly-cold-outreach-comparison).)

### 6. Sopro — best for UK & European markets

A well-known UK-based, email-led outreach provider with a productised model and strong European market coverage. Starting price around $3,800/month.

- **Strengths**: strong UK/EU data and compliance; established brand; productised delivery.
- **Watch-outs**: email-led, lighter multichannel; pricing reflects the UK market.
- **Pick them if**: your core market is the UK or Europe.

### 7. Callbox — best for enterprise & global reach

A high-touch lead generation operation combining email, calling, and LinkedIn at scale, built for enterprise and global markets.

- **Strengths**: enterprise-grade; global coverage; multi-channel with calling depth.
- **Watch-outs**: enterprise pricing and process; heavier for a small team.
- **Pick them if**: you're an enterprise running global, multi-region outbound.

### 8. OutreachBloom — best for pure email, no SDR overhead

A focused, done-for-you cold email shop handling the full pipeline — ICP, lists, domains, sequences, and reply management — without the cost of dedicated SDRs.

- **Strengths**: email specialist; lean model; full DFY pipeline.
- **Watch-outs**: email only — no LinkedIn or calling.
- **Pick them if**: you want pure email, done well, without paying for channels you won't use.

### 9. UltraGrowthMedia — best managed email for mid-market

A result-driven managed service running cold email end-to-end — ICP, sourcing, copy, A/B testing, deliverability, and reply handling — with ICP-specific lists rather than scraped databases.

- **Strengths**: fully managed; custom list building; proven across 50+ clients with a 27-person team.
- **Watch-outs**: email-focused; mid-to-high pricing.
- **Pick them if**: you're mid-market and want a hands-off managed email program.
